Quick answers

Questions before you start

Do I need the Photo Enlarger to draw from photos?
For a printed photo, yes — the LUCY reflects whatever is in front of it, so you pair it with the Photo Enlarger accessory ($22) to magnify the photo 2.5–4× to drawing size. For a live subject right in front of you — your dog, a vase of flowers, a person sitting for a portrait — you don’t need any accessory. We list the Photo Enlarger right next to the models above so it’s easy to add.
Will my drawing actually look like the photo?
The LUCY gives you accurate placement and proportion — the part that usually makes a likeness go wrong — so you start from a strong, recognizable base. The shading and finishing are yours, and results vary and improve with practice. A relaxed warm-up pass on a printed copy before your keeper makes a real difference.
Is using it cheating?
It’s a recognized learning aid, not a crutch — you still draw every line yourself. Optical drawing aids like the camera lucida and camera obscura have been associated with master artists for centuries; the LUCY is a modern take on the same idea. Over time it trains your eye for proportion, which is the skill most self-taught artists struggle with.
I wear glasses — will it work?
Single-vision glasses work fine. Bifocals or progressives can sometimes make the reflected image look doubled, because you’re looking at two distances at once. The easy fix: wear inexpensive single-vision reading glasses while you draw, or remove your glasses if you can focus at drawing distance without them.
