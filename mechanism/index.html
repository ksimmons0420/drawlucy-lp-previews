Questions, answered

What people ask before they order

Isn’t this cheating?
Not at all — it’s the same kind of optical aid the Old Masters have long been associated with. Da Vinci, Vermeer, and Ingres are all linked to optical drawing aids (the camera lucida and camera obscura). Drawing over the reflected image helps you learn proportion and trains your eye; over time, many artists rely on it less and less. It’s a learning tool, not a shortcut around the real skill.
Do I need any drawing skill?
No prior skill is needed to get started — if you can hold a pencil and draw over a line, you can use the LUCY. Your results will naturally improve the more you practice. Results vary from person to person and with how much you practice.
What can I draw with it?
Anything you can place in front of it or display on a screen: photos, portraits, pets, landscapes, still-life objects, reference images, even other artwork. Use any medium — pencil, pen, charcoal, marker, or paint — on virtually any surface.
Which model should I get?
Most people go with the LUCY flex ($99) — it’s our most popular and most versatile, with a bigger image and four brightness levels for nearly any subject. Choose the LUCY mini ($69) if you want the budget option or work at smaller sizes. The LUCY pro ($275) is the largest and most stable, made for serious and professional work.
