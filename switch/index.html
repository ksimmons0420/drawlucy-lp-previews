Straight answers

What switchers ask before they buy

How is this faster than a projector?
A projector throws a beam of light, so it needs power, a flat surface to align to, and a darkened room — and it washes out in daylight. The LUCY uses optics to overlay a reflected image straight onto your paper, so it works at an ordinary, well-lit desk with no cords and roughly a 30-second setup. It also draws from live 3D subjects, which a projector can’t.
Is it really faster than gridding by hand?
For most people, yes. You skip ruling the grid onto both the reference and the paper and counting squares to transfer it. You clamp the LUCY, look through it, and trace the placement directly. Results vary, and there’s a short (~30-minute) learning curve to dial in your eye position and brightness — after that, the drawing is quick.
Will the accuracy be as good as the grid?
The LUCY transfers exact placement, so proportions land where they should — comparable to a carefully counted grid. The difference is the time and setup it takes to get there. It helps you see. You still draw every single stroke yourself.
Does it work in normal room light?
Yes — no dark room required. Because it shows a reflection, lighting does matter: light your subject well and keep your paper a touch dimmer. The flex and pro include multiple brightness levels to dial the image in. No blackout blinds, unlike a projector.
I wear glasses — will it work?
Single-vision glasses work normally. Progressives or bifocals can sometimes make the image appear doubled because you’re looking at two focal distances at once — the fix is inexpensive single-vision reading glasses, or removing your glasses if you can focus at drawing distance.
