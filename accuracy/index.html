Honest answers

Before you decide

Isn’t using it cheating? Will I still learn?
No — it’s a recognized learning aid. Optical drawing aids like the camera lucida have been associated with master artists for centuries. The LUCY does the seeing; you still draw every stroke yourself. Over time it trains your eye for proportion — the exact skill that’s hardest to build by feel.
I tried the grid and freehand — why is this different?
Freehand leaves placement to judgment, so small errors compound. The grid helps but is tedious and a single mis-plotted square still drifts. The LUCY shows the actual subject in correct proportion right on your paper, so placement is locked from the first line — then the artistry is all yours.
Is there a learning curve?
A little — about 30 minutes to position the mirror, get your eye in the right spot, and dial in the brightness. Give it one short practice session before your “real” drawing and you’ll skip the only frustration people report. Results vary and improve with practice.
I wear glasses or bifocals — will it work?
Single-vision glasses work normally. Progressive or bifocal lenses can sometimes make the image look doubled — the easy fix is inexpensive single-vision reading glasses while you draw, or removing your glasses if you can focus at drawing distance without them.
